# CLA privacy notice

Effective 13 July 2026

This notice applies to personal data collected when contributors sign the TiMini-Print Contributor License Agreement (CLA).

## Controller and contact

Daniel Banecki is the controller of this data. Questions and data-protection requests can be sent to `cla@wtrymiga.pl`.

## Data collected

The signing record may contain Your GitHub account and numeric account ID, full name, email address, signing capacity, acceptance response, signature timestamp, CLA version, and identifiers of related pull requests or commits.

## Purpose and legal basis

This data is used to verify who accepted the CLA, administer contributions, maintain reliable licensing records, and establish, exercise, or defend legal claims. Processing is necessary to perform the CLA at Your request and for the legitimate interests of keeping accurate records of rights granted to the Project. Providing the required signing data is voluntary, but a Contribution cannot be accepted without a reliable CLA record.

## Services and transfers

GitHub and the hosted CLA Assistant service process signing data on behalf of, or independently from, the Project Owner as their roles require. CLA Assistant is operated by SAP and reports that its signature database is hosted in Microsoft Azure in Europe. GitHub and its service providers may process data outside Poland or the European Economic Area under the safeguards described in their privacy terms. Their own notices also apply: [GitHub Privacy Statement](https://docs.github.com/en/site-policy/privacy-policies/github-general-privacy-statement) and [CLA Assistant privacy statement](https://gist.github.com/CLAassistant/3a73e4cd729c9d0a6e30).

## Retention

Signing records are kept for as long as Contributions or rights granted under the CLA may be used, and afterward where reasonably necessary to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims or meet legal obligations. Removing public account information does not necessarily allow the underlying licensing record to be deleted.

## Your rights

Subject to applicable law, You may request access, correction, restriction, objection, portability, or deletion of Your personal data. Some requests may be limited where the record is needed to document the CLA or defend legal claims. You may also complain to the Polish data-protection authority, the [President of the Personal Data Protection Office](https://uodo.gov.pl/en), or another competent supervisory authority.

The signing process does not make decisions about You through profiling. Its automated effect is limited to reporting whether the required CLA acceptance has been recorded for a contribution.
